DNA methylation (DNAm)




  • CpG - most popular methylation site

  • non-uniformly distributed

  • ‘marker’ for gene activation

  • DNA methylation is a methyl-group attached to DNA;
  • in humans, methylation occurs primarily on a cytosine in a CpG dinucleotide;
  • these CpGs are not uniformly distributed on the genome and the methylation level marks whether the genes are activated or disactivated

mQTL

methylation quantitative locus

SNP–CpG pair:

association between
methylation status of that CpG and
allele at that SNP


  • cis-mQTLs and trans-mQTLs

  • mQTL is a methylation quantitative locus;

  • an association between SNP and CpG where the change in the alleles on the SNP are correlated with the change in methylation status of CpG;

  • this association indicates the functional connection between alleles and methylation, which in reality is probably mediated through various proteins;

  • depending on the distance between the SNP and CpG in an mQTL, we have cis and trans;

DNAm reprogramming & ART

ART = assisted reproductive technology

More and more people postpone the creation of family and thus often when they decide on having a child it might be too late.

ART were in use since 1980’s and are well tested and safe procedure. However, we still don’t know details on how it’s affecting the biological processes that take place in the first days of embryonic development.

The ART procedures require, namely, extracting the sperm and eggs, and growing the fertilized embryos on a petri dish in 3-5 days. After that, some embryos are frozen and the best is transferred to the woman.

methods

  1. remove outliers and un-reliable CpGs![*]

  2. agnostic search for mQTLs in parents

  3. select the significant mQTLs

  4. check for association in children using three models:

  • DNAm \(\sim\) SNP \(+\) case_control_status
  • DNAm \(\sim\) SNP \(\times\) case_control_status
  • DNAm \(\sim\) SNP, stratified by case_control_status

[*] Sugden et al., Patterns, 2020 (DOI:10.1016/j.patter.2020.100014)
